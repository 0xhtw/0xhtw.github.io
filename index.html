about me!

Hi, I’m 0xhtw. At least that is what I go by when publishing my works online. I love computers, cars, music, art and most importantly anything cybersecurity. I started from a young age and I am dedicated to persistent self development and improvement, sharing my findings in things such as malware analysis, reverse engineering and threat intelligence. I dropped out of school from a young age to pursue a career within IT, but I always knew that I was destined for the infosec field as growing up I had a natural talent for learning anything hacking and malware related, it didn't take long before I got into trouble at school for shutting all the computers off on the domain. I won't go into that too much but that's how I knew I wanted to put all my efforts into cybersecurity. I have a deep passion for it, it's not a career path I desire just to make money, it is something I enjoy doing and continue to improve on in my own personal time. You'll see that in the section below talking on what htw is.

what is htw?

HTW stands for Hack The World — began in Milton Keynes, a city with a reputation for pioneering tech and bold urban design, what started as a small artistic collective evolved into a small group of people passionate about creativity, technology and exploration in both urban and digital senses.

HTW exists across different spaces, both online and offline. Representing our crossover between urban street art culture and technology. It's more than a crew, it's a mindset of exploration, experimentation, and pushing boundaries.

That philosophy guides my work in cybersecurity: the drive to learn, to innovate and share knowledge in ways that make systems stronger and uncover new findings in the forever evolving world of cybersecurity.
